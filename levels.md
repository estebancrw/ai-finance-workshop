# The 5 Levels of Personal AI

A maturity ladder for how people use AI on their own life — personal finance
here, but the ladder is domain-agnostic (health, career, business all climb
the same way).

```
Level 0 — Stateless chat        "generic advice, from scratch, every time"
Level 1 — Snapshot KB           "AI that knows my numbers and my rules"
Level 2 — Living KB             "AI that knows my history"
Level 3 — Connected KB          "AI that stays current by itself"
Level 4 — Agentic               "AI that acts — inside guardrails"
```

## Level 0 — Stateless chat

Open a tab, ask, get an answer that would fit anyone, close the tab.
Nothing persists. **What breaks:** everything personal — the AI can't know
your debt rate, your risk tolerance, or your rules, so it hedges with
"it depends" and lists.

**Upgrade trigger:** the first time you notice you're re-explaining your
situation for the third time.

## Level 1 — Snapshot KB  ← the workshop gets you here

Engine + cartridge in a saved Project/Gem. The AI reads your numbers and
your non-negotiables before every answer. Unlocks: *am I protected? where
can I cut? where does my next peso go?* — answered with your data, and your
own rules enforced back at you.

**What breaks:** numbers go stale. A snapshot from March is a lie in July.

**Upgrade trigger:** the first time the AI's answer is wrong because the
snapshot was old.

## Level 2 — Living KB

You add a tiny ritual: 15 minutes, once a month — refresh `snapshot.md`,
bump the `as_of` date, add one rule you learned about yourself. Now the KB
has *history*, and trend questions unlock: *am I better off than last
month? is my "misc" shrinking? did the plan survive contact with December?*

This is where compound value lives. No generic chatbot can ever answer a
trend question about you — only your own KB can.

**What breaks:** manual effort. You are the sync mechanism.

**Upgrade trigger:** the ritual is a habit and the copy-paste starts to
feel dumb.

## Level 3 — Connected KB

The KB stays current with less manual work: calendar knows payment dates,
email receives the statements, files hold the history — connected via your
AI tool's integrations. The monthly refresh becomes review-and-confirm
instead of collect-and-type.

**What breaks:** requires paid tiers, integration setup, and real thought
about what you're granting access to.

**Upgrade trigger:** you trust the system enough to want it proactive.

## Level 4 — Agentic

The AI doesn't just advise — it *acts*: drafts the transfer, schedules the
payment, negotiates the renewal. This is where the industry is heading.

**The non-negotiable at this level: approval gates.** In 2024, Air
Canada's support chatbot confidently invented a bereavement-refund policy —
and a Canadian tribunal ruled the airline had to honor what its bot
promised. The AI acted; the human paid. The lesson isn't "never automate" —
it's **draft vs. execute**: AI drafts, human approves, for anything
irreversible (money moved, message sent, commitment made).

A practical trust rule: keep approving every action manually until the
AI's error rate drops below roughly 1 in 5 — only then start loosening
the gates, one action type at a time.

## Where the tools are today

OpenAI shipped exactly this pattern as a product in May 2026 (ChatGPT
personal finance: bank connections, spending dashboard, grounded answers) —
currently US-only and Pro-tier. This repo is the same idea, built by hand:
free, tool-agnostic, works anywhere, and you keep custody of your data.
